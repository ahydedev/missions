Become an Olympic Hero

Paris needs 45,000 volunteers to deliver the greatest sporting event on Earth

The 2024 Olympic Games will take place in Paris and supporting cities between 26 July and 11 August 2024, with the 2024 Paralympic Games taking place between 28 August and 8 September 2024.

Some 15,000 athletes are expected to compete, and millions of spectators will gather in over 40 competition sites to witness first-hand a sporting event the likes of which the world has never seen before.

As a volunteer, you can be part of a unique team adventure at the heart of these events, making new connections with people from around the world and helping to make Paris 2024 an unforgettable event.

Be more than a spectator, be part of the Games!

A game-changing opportunity

As an Olympic volunteer you'll be at the heart of the organisation of the greatest sporting event on the planet and directly contributing to its success. You'll welcome the whole world, embodying the hospitality of France and the team spirit of the Games, and sharing unique moments in a truly intercultural exchange.

Volunteer missions will take place in over 40 Olympic venues, including in Paris and Île-de-France (Paris region), Marseille, Lille, Nice, Lyon, Saint-Etienne, Nantes, Bordeaux, Châteauroux and Tahiti. They will also take place in other key locations such as the Media Centre, Athletes' Village, training facilities, train stations and airports.

Placements are flexible with time commitments from 1 week to 4 weeks in duration. Each placement offers a unique opportunity to connect with brilliant people who have a passion for sport, and to learn, train and gain experience that may be useful for your future career plans.

As a volunteer you'll also enjoy free tickets to the Games, an official Olympic uniform and access to discounts on accommodation.

Missions

Find the perfect mission

Three types of missions will be assigned to volunteers. The first is focused on delivering a quality experience to participants at the Games, including spectators, athletes, sport delegations and journalists. Volunteers will welcome, guide, inform, transport and assist them during their mission period.

The second is focused on sporting performance. These volunteers will support the athletes in the competition area by helping them to install starting blocks, look after the belongings of swimmers, retrieve tennis balls, and more. They will also help with tasks such as the measuring of timings and statistics, and supporting the medical staff in the Athletes' village and in the competition/training areas.

The final missions will be focused on organisation, with tasks that may include assisting with the accreditation process, or distributing and installing equipment. You'll be supported with virtual and face-to-face training and orientation in advance of the events.


How to apply

You can view all current opportunities and apply for your preferred mission(s) via the Opportunities page. Recruitment is now live for the July 2024 Paris Olympics and opportunities for the September 2024 Paralympic Games will be posted from June 2023.

You'll need to fill in a short form outlining your interest in the placement and providing any appropriate details regarding your skill set and/or experience. To help us to identify a suitable placement, you will also be asked to confirm your avaiability and the location(s) at which you are able to serve as a volunteer.

The responsible Mission Manager will review your application and contact you within 60 days with an update on your application. If you are selected to support the Games, you will be provided with follow up guidance and preparatory support.

Volunteers will need to be 18 years old on or before 1 January 2024, and speak either French or English. Missions will last at least ten days and will take place between July and September 2024. Volunteers can be citizens of any country in the world.

Volunteer at Rio 2016

"There were 10,000 of us who were volunteer performers at the London 2012 Opening Ceremony and it will forever make me smile that no details were leaked despite immense curiosity, particularly from national newspapers, of how the UK would be showcased to the world. More than 900 million viewers across the globe tuned in, with 80,000 people including the Queen in the stadium so it needed to be quite the show."

Volunteer dancer at London 2012

The Olympic feeling takes over the city, the barriers between countries fall. Everybody is excited because there is a common goal: the Olympics!"

Volunteer at Rio 2016
